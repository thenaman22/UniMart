# UniMart: Non-Technical Overview

## What UniMart Is

UniMart is a private, community-first marketplace. Instead of posting items in a public marketplace or a noisy group chat, people buy and sell inside trusted communities where membership is approved.

That makes it a strong fit for places where trust, relevance, and access control matter, such as campuses, neighborhood sale groups, clubs, departments, and internal surplus programs.

## What the App Does

UniMart helps people:

- join the right communities
- browse items that are relevant to their group
- post items for sale
- manage their own listings
- view seller profiles inside trusted shared communities
- send listing-based inquiries
- keep buying and selling organized instead of scattered across random chats

At the same time, community owners and moderators can manage who gets in, who can post, and how the marketplace is run.

## Main Features

- Private community access through approved email domains, invite links, or access requests
- Community-only listings, so items are shown to the right audience instead of the whole internet
- Searchable marketplace feed for discovering relevant items faster
- Seller profiles that are only visible to people who share an active community with that seller
- Listing management tools to create, edit, relist, mark sold, remove, or delete items
- Photo and media support so buyers can better understand what is being sold
- Moderation tools for reviewing access requests, member issues, and reports
- Self-serve community creation, so the app is not limited to a fixed set of marketplaces
- Posting rules per community, so each community can decide whether:
  - all members can post
  - only approved sellers can post
  - only the creator/admin can post
- Different roles inside a community, such as viewer, seller, moderator, and admin
- Community management tools like invite generation, allowed email domains, member review, and role assignment
- Listing-based messaging/inquiries, so conversations stay tied to the item instead of getting lost in unrelated chats

## Use Cases

### Universities

UniMart works well for university campuses where students need a safer and more relevant way to buy and sell:

- textbooks
- bikes
- furniture
- electronics
- hostel or dorm items
- club or event materials

Instead of posting in a giant public marketplace, students can trade inside a campus-only or department-specific community. Access can be controlled through university email domains, invite links, or moderator approval.

### Garage Sales and Neighborhood Sales

UniMart can also power local garage-sale style communities:

- apartment buildings
- residential societies
- neighborhood groups
- temporary event-based sale groups

People get a cleaner, more organized space than a chat group. Organizers can control membership, reduce spam, and keep buyers focused on actual listings.

### Surplus Sales

For universities, labs, startups, offices, or departments, UniMart can support internal surplus and resale:

- old furniture
- office equipment
- lab materials
- event leftovers
- reusable supplies

This is especially useful when only certain people should be allowed to post, approve members, or manage the sale process.

## Why UniMart Is Better Than WhatsApp Groups

WhatsApp is useful for conversation, but it is not designed to run a marketplace well.

- Posts get buried quickly in ongoing chat.
- There is no clean listing format for title, price, condition, images, and status.
- It is hard to search old items properly.
- Sold items remain mixed with active ones.
- Buyer questions move into scattered personal chats.
- Group admins do not get strong marketplace-specific moderation tools.

UniMart improves this by keeping listings structured, searchable, and tied to communities, seller profiles, and moderation rules.

## Why UniMart Is Better Than Facebook Marketplace

Facebook Marketplace is broad and public. UniMart is focused and trust-based.

- Facebook Marketplace exposes items to a very wide audience.
- UniMart keeps access limited to approved members of a relevant community.
- Facebook is better for general public selling; UniMart is better for closed ecosystems.
- UniMart lets each community define who can join, who can post, and who can moderate.
- UniMart is a better fit for campuses, organizations, clubs, private sale groups, and internal reuse programs.

## Simple Comparison

| Option | Main Problem | Why UniMart Is Better |
| --- | --- | --- |
| WhatsApp groups | Unstructured, noisy, and hard to search | UniMart gives structured listings, seller profiles, listing status, and moderation |
| Facebook Marketplace | Too public and not community-specific | UniMart is private, trust-based, and community-controlled |

## In One Line

UniMart is a safer, more organized, and more trustworthy way for closed communities to buy and sell than using public marketplaces or informal chat groups.
